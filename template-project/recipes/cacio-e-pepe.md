# Cacio e Pepe

Three ingredients. No cream. No shortcuts. Just pasta, cheese, and pepper — done right.

## Ingredients

- 400g spaghetti or tonnarelli
- 200g Pecorino Romano, finely grated
- 2 tablespoons whole black peppercorns
- Salt for the pasta water

## Steps

1. Toast the peppercorns in a dry skillet until fragrant. Crush them coarsely with a mortar and pestle (or the bottom of a pan).
2. Boil the pasta in well-salted water. Cook until just under al dente — it finishes cooking in the pan.
3. While the pasta cooks, add the crushed pepper to a large cold skillet with a splash of olive oil. Warm it gently.
4. Reserve at least 2 cups of starchy pasta water before draining.
5. Add about a cup of pasta water to the pepper pan. Bring to a simmer.
6. Toss the pasta into the pan. Cook for a minute, tossing constantly, adding splashes of pasta water as needed.
7. Remove from heat. Wait 30 seconds. Then add the Pecorino in handfuls, tossing vigorously. The residual heat and starchy water create the sauce. If it's too thick, add more pasta water. If it's too thin, add more cheese.
8. Serve immediately. More pepper and cheese on top.

## Notes

The trick is temperature control. Too hot and the cheese clumps. Too cold and it won't melt. Off the heat with vigorous tossing is the sweet spot.
