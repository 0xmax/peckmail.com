# Beef Bourguignon

A rich, hearty French stew that fills the whole house with the most incredible smell. Best made on a lazy Sunday.

## Ingredients

- 1 kg beef chuck, cut into 3cm cubes
- 150g bacon lardons
- 1 bottle red Burgundy wine
- 2 tablespoons tomato paste
- 3 carrots, peeled and sliced
- 1 large onion, diced
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 250g small mushrooms, halved
- 12 pearl onions, peeled
- 2 tablespoons flour
- 2 cups beef stock
- Fresh thyme and bay leaves
- Salt and pepper
- 2 tablespoons butter
- Olive oil

## Steps

1. Pat the beef dry and season generously with salt and pepper.
2. Brown the beef in batches in a hot Dutch oven with olive oil. Don't crowd the pan — you want a deep sear. Set aside.
3. In the same pot, cook the lardons until crispy. Add the diced onion and carrots. Cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
4. Stir in the garlic and tomato paste. Cook for a minute until fragrant.
5. Sprinkle the flour over the vegetables and stir well.
6. Pour in the entire bottle of wine. Yes, the whole thing. Add the beef stock.
7. Return the beef to the pot. Add thyme and bay leaves.
8. Cover and cook in the oven at 160C for 2.5 to 3 hours.
9. In the last 30 minutes, sauté the mushrooms and pearl onions in butter and add them to the stew.
10. Taste and adjust seasoning. Serve over mashed potatoes or with crusty bread.

## Notes

This is even better the next day. The flavors deepen overnight in the fridge.
