# Worldbuilding Notes — The Vela Gap Setting

## The big picture

Humanity spread to the stars about 300 years ago, but not as conquerors — more like careful tenants. The Terran Exploratory Fleet maps and surveys, but colonization is slow and cautious. Most people still live on Earth or in the orbital stations.

The Vela Gap is a region of unusually low star density between the Vela and Puppis constellations. Nobody goes there because there's nothing there. Or so everyone thought.

## Factions

### Terran Exploratory Fleet (TEF)
The main spacefaring body. Part navy, part research institute. Ships are named after birds (Meadowlark, Kingfisher, Peregrine, Curlew). Crews are small, missions are long. Culture is informal and close-knit.

### Cygnus Station
The last outpost before the Vela Gap. Part fuel depot, part trading post, part bar. Everyone passes through eventually. Run by a retired Fleet captain named Devi who knows everyone and remembers everything.

### The Archive Collective
A loose network of academics and independent researchers who believe the galaxy was once home to a much older civilization. They've been largely dismissed as cranks. The Meadowlark's discovery is about to change that.

## The Builders

Whatever left the structure in the Vela Gap. We don't know what they looked like, where they went, or why they left their library floating in the dark. The glass spheres might hold the answer, but we don't know how to play them yet.

Working theories:
- They transcended physical form (Fen's favorite)
- They migrated to another galaxy (Ren's theory)
- They're still here, just somewhere we can't see (Mo's unsettling suggestion)

## Technology

FTL travel exists but is slow and expensive. A trip across human-explored space takes weeks. Communication uses quantum-entangled relay stations, with about 1 day of latency per relay hop.

Ships are powered by compact fusion reactors. Weapons exist but are rarely used — the TEF is not a military force, and there's nobody to fight (yet).

## Themes

- The loneliness of deep space and the bonds that form in isolation
- Finding evidence of something vast and ancient and trying to understand it
- The tension between exploration and preservation
- Small crews doing extraordinary things far from home
