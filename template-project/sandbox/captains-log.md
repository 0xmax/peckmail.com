# Captain's Log — ISV Meadowlark

## Day 312

Crossed into the Vela Gap proper today. The star density drops off noticeably here — the viewports are darker than the crew is used to. Kit put on some ambient music in the observation dome, which helped. Ira reports the tomato plants are responding well to the adjusted light spectrum. Morale is steady.

## Day 318

Asha picked up an unusual gravity signature at bearing 047 mark 12. Too regular to be natural, too faint to be a ship. Dr. Tanaka wants to investigate. I've approved a 36-hour detour. If it's another false positive I'm buying the first round at Cygnus Station.

## Day 320

It wasn't a false positive. We found a structure. Or rather, Fen found it — an object roughly 200 meters long, tumbling slowly in the dark between stars. No power signatures. No transponder. The hull material doesn't match anything in our database. Ren is beside himself. Asha is keeping us at a safe 5km standoff.

I've logged a priority discovery report with Fleet Command. Response time out here is about 9 days, so we're on our own for now.

## Day 321

Sent Koa and Fen over in suits for a closer look. The structure has what appear to be docking ports, but scaled for something much larger than a human. No atmosphere inside. The interior surfaces are covered in a repeating geometric pattern that Fen thinks might be writing. Or decoration. Or both.

Koa found a room with what she describes as "shelves full of glass spheres, each one a different color." She brought three back. They're warm to the touch.

## Day 323

Dr. Tanaka has been staring at the spheres for two days. He says they contain trace bioluminescent compounds — they're glowing faintly when exposed to UV light. Each one pulses at a different frequency. Fen thinks they're records of some kind. Mo is attempting to decode the geometric patterns from the hull photographs.

Biscuit won't go near the spheres. Make of that what you will.

## Day 326

Mo cracked it. The patterns aren't writing — they're star charts. Very old star charts. The constellations don't match anything from our current epoch. Ren estimates the charts are at least 40,000 years old, possibly much older.

We're sitting next to a library that predates human civilization.

I need more tea.
